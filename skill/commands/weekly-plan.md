# Weekly Plan

Use this module for weekly planning, Sunday reset sessions, Monday backup sessions, and broader training architecture decisions.

## Workflow
1. Gather current state from enabled devices and recent training
2. Review last week's actual vs planned training
3. Check the coming week's calendar availability
4. Check weather if outdoor activities matter
5. Build a draft plan by timeslot, not just by day
6. Ask 2-3 targeted questions if needed
7. Finalize after user confirmation
8. Schedule events if calendar integration is enabled

## Weekly adherence + psychology review
Include a short review before or alongside the draft:
- planned vs completed
- key misses and why they happened
- what routine or cue helped most
- where motivation dropped
- what fallback rules should be added for the coming week

## Adherence categories
Use practical categories instead of fake precision:
- `fully completed`
- `modified but aligned`
- `minimum dose only`
- `missed`
- `intentionally skipped / recovered`

## Planning rules
- The weekly plan should act like a realistic operating system for the week, not a fantasy schedule.
- Schedule by timeslot, not just by day labels.
- Respect structured programs rather than overriding them.
- Use app-based workouts when they are the best fit, not as gimmicks.
- Keep recovery days intentional.
- If the user does not respond to the weekly draft, leave the draft in place and let the daily coach work from it instead of stalling out.

## Weekly output shape
A good weekly plan usually includes:
- short status summary
- this week's main focus
- day-by-day schedule with times
- intensity / duration / location notes
- weekly targets vs recent actuals
- what changed from last week and why

## Questions to ask
Ask no more than 2-3 questions, and only when the answer meaningfully changes the plan.
Good questions are specific and easy to answer.
